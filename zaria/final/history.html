History

Scientific classification

Before learning about the history of the fishing cat, let's learn some about the fishing cat's Scientific classification!

The fishing cat belongs to P. viverrinus species and the Prionailurus genus, which was proposed in 1858 as a name for spotted amd small wild cats from Asia.

Phylogeny

The Prionailurus species are estimated to have had a common ancestor between 8.16 to 4.53 million years ago. The fishing cat has been the third cat of the Prionailurus lineage that genetically diverged.

History

Fishing cat is the common name for the animal. It also has other names and is introduced below!

In 1833, Felis viverrinus, the first name for the fishing cat, was proposed by Edward Turner Bennett, who described a fishing cat skin on a human-dominated wetland habitat of Mid-West Bengal in India.

in 1936, Felis viverrinus rhizophoreus was proposed by Henri Jacob Victor Sody, who described a specimen from the north coast of West Java. However, the specimen had a slightly shorter skull than fishing cat specimens from Thailand.
