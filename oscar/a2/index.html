S'mores Cookie Recipe

Introduction

I chose this recipe because I really like s’mores and cookies, so I decided to put them together. I enjoy roasting marshmallows by a fire so I thought it would taste good in one of the most common snacks known. With this recipe, I can have a campfire classic in a cookie.

Ingredients

  • 1 Hershey’s Chocolate Bar
  • 1 cup of mini-Marshmallows
  • 4 Graham Crackers
  • ¼ tsp of Salt 
  • 1 Egg
  • 1 tsp of vanilla extract
  • ½ cup of brown sugar
  • ¼ cup of granulated sugar
  • 10 tbsp of unsalted butter
  • ¾ cup of unsweetened chocolate chip cookies
  • 1 tsp of corn starch
  • ½ tsp of baking powder
  • ½ tsp of baking soda
  • 1½ cup of all-purpose flour

Instructions

  1. Roughly chop the Graham crackers and Hershey's bar, then tear the mini marshmallows in half. These marshmallows are one of the best parts of the cookie, but they will puff up and get a bit out of control if you don't halve them.
  2. Combine the flour, salt, baking powder, baking soda, and cornstarch in a bowl then whisk together and set aside.
  3. Using a stand or hand mixer, beat butter and sugar in a bowl until creamy, about 2 minutes. Add egg and vanilla and until combined. Scrape the bowl down and mix once more.
  4. Gradually add flour mixture to butter mixture, beating at low speed until just combined.
  5. Reserve a handful of the torn marshmallows and set aside then add the remaining marshmallows, chocolate chips, and Graham crackers to the dough and mix on low until just combined (do not over-beat).
  6. Refrigerate the dough for an hour or more.
  7. Heat oven to 300°C. Portion out roughly two tablespoon sized pieces of dough. Roll into balls and place on a parchment-lined baking sheet, spacing about 2 inches apart.
  8. Bake at 300°C for 8 minutes then top the cookies with the reserved marshmallow and Hershey's bar pieces then return to the oven for 2-3 minutes to finish baking. If some cookies had a blowout because of a few too many marshmallows too you can corral them back together with a round cookie cutter or a knife.
  9. Leave on pan to cool completely.
