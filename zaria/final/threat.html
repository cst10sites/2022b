Threat and Conservation

Threat

Only a little is know about the fishing cat, but it is thought that it has no natural predators, other than humans.

However, the fishing cat is threatened by destruction of wetlands, which are its preferred habitat. As a result of human settlements and agricultural uses, many wetlands are increasingly polluted.

Besides the polluted wetlands, the depletion of fish also threatens the fishing cat.

Many fishing cats are killed in different places around the world.

  • In West Bengal's Howrah district, between April 2010 and May 2011, 27 fishing cat died.
  • In Bangladesh, between January 2010 and March 2013, at least 30 fishing cats were killed by local people.
  • In a study in Thailand, 84% of fishing cats that were tracked via radio collars were killed.
  • The fishing cat is possibly extinct in coastal Kerala, India.

As a result, its conservation status is endangered.

Conservation

The fishing cat is mostly protected by national legislation. For example, hunting is prohibited in many countries.

The fishing cat captive breeding programmes are also established, including the European Association of Zoos and Aquaria and the American Association of Zoos and Aquariums.
