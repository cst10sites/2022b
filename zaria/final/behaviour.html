Behaviour

Activity Time and Territory

The fishing cat is thought to be a kind of nocturnal animal. During the day, it rests among dense vegetation and then at night heads to the near water to find food.

Adult males and females without dependent young are solitary. Both of them have their territories. They occupy home ranges as large as 22 sq. km. Usually, the male’s territories are border than several different females. They live alone, and they spend their nights quietly foraging in their territories.


Swimming

Unlike many cats, the fishing cat swims. With its special front toes and claws, it can swim long distances, even under water.

Diet

The fishing cat is generalist, so it feeds on what is available. Primarily, it eats small mammals and fish. In the wild, the fishing cat catches fish at the edge of bodies of water by scooping with its paws. In shallow water, it has been observed playing with fish in shallow water. However, its teeth are not specially modified for catching fish, so its diet often includes some small to medium-sized vertebrate it catches.

Besides fish, it sometimes eat birds, as well as other small prey, including lizards. In addition, it is fed on livestock, especially poultry. The fishing cat can also kill frogs and snakes; also rodents, coots, ducks and sandpipers; also a variety of domestic animals, including goats and calves.
