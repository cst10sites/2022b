Album Reviews and Ratings

    Wake


    Wake is a Post-Hardcore album released in 2014 by the band: Hail The sun.
    In the wide genre that is Post-Hardcore, Hail The Sun is far more "Emo"
    than other bands. Its sound ranges from melodic to loud and angstey.
    The lead singers piercing voice and their lead guitarists simple but haunting riffs left me stunned on this album.
    Highlight tracks include:

      Jane Doe
      Human Target Practice
      Anti Eulogy (I hope you stay dead)

    8.4/10



    Remain In Light


    This 1980 New wuave LP by The Talking Heads is a personal favourite of mine.
    It incorporates multiple styles of music including: African drums, Funk Rock and Experimental (or Avant Garde) Rock.
    This album is nothing less than a work of art, from Tina Weymouth's bass playing, to
    David Byrne's astounding songwriting and vocal preformance. This album is nothing but amazing.
    Highlight tracks include:

      Seen But Not Seen
      Born Under Punches (And The Heat Goes On)
      Once In A Lifetime

    9.6/10



    Rage Against The Machine


    This 1991 LP is (despite not being a punk album) the most mainstream punk rock album. Zach De La Rocha
    shows off his aggressive rap-metal voice really solidify the name of the band. Tom Morello's guitar
    work is absoulutely astounding with sounds I didnt know could be made on a guitar. Songs
    become a little repititive sounding after a while, but the album is overall good.
    Higlight Tracks Include:

      Township Rebellion
      Know Your Enemy
      Bombtrack

    8.1/10



    Dirt


    This 1992 Album was released a year after Nirvana's:Nevermind and didnt come to as commercial success,
    but was seen as the magnum opus of the Seattle grunge band; Alice In Chains.
    I love this album despite it having multiple misses and a few downright annoying songs.
    Overall pretty solid and good album
    Higlight Tracks Include:

      Rain When I Die
      Junkhead
      Dirt

    7.2/10



    Power, Corruption & Lies


    A 1983 Post-Punk classic from the band New Order which formed out of the
    legendary band Joy Division after their singer tragically died. This album is nothing less than astoundinly amazing, incorperating:
    Synth Rock, New Wave, Dance rock and Post-Punk masterfully. The guitar work and Lyrics are bold and perfect
    There's really nothing else I can say.
    Higlight Tracks Include:

      Age Of Consent
      Village
      5 8 6

    9.7/10



    For The First Time


    This 2021 debut from British Post-Rock band: Black Country, New Road and they truly came out swingin'.
    The vocals on this album sound akin to a teenage boy with a deep voice about to crack at any time
    but it does not. The vocals are great, the drumming is absoulutely amazing and the almost harmonious instrumentation
    leaves my jaw on the floor every time i listen. although this is a great album, songs go on for a little too long
    without much change. I personally prefer there 2022 Lp: Ants From Up There
    Highlight Tracks Include:

      Science Fair
      Athens, France
      Sunglasses

    7.8/10



    Some Rap Songs


    This 2018 album released by rap artist Earl Sweatshirt is a personal favorite of mine.0000000000000000000000
    For this album sounds like nothing I've ever heard before, and Earls lyrics alongside his droney, yet calming
    voice is a pleasure to listen to. As well as the experimental jazz like instrumentation that sounds grainy yet modern.
    This album does have a few stinker & skips on it that sound less good than the other songs and feel like
    an attempt at being experimental.
    Highlight Tracks Include:

      Riot!
      Shattered Dreams
      Azucar

    8.3/10



    Damned Damned Damned



    This is my favorite punk album to date. This 1979 LP by The Damned.
    Truly there's not much I can say, this album has the perfect punk.
    guitar, vocals and drumming. Songs are short, sweet and aggressive
    which is about as much as i can ask for. Gold Star from me.
    Highlight Tracks Include:

      Neat Neat Neat
      Fan Club
      Stab Yor Back

    9.2/10



    The Bends



    Radioheads sophomore album released in 1995 in my opinion was there most "conventional"
    album. The guitar work is great, but nothing unseen in the 90s alternitave seen at the time.
    The lyricsism is a real highlight for Thom York was truly coming into himself artisically.
    Solid album, accessable, and an easy listen.
    Highlight Tracks Include:

      PLanet Telex
      Fake Plastic Trees
      Just

    8.4/10



    Metallica (The Black Album)



    This is a not great album. Is it bad? No but in comparison between every album in the 90s and Metallica's albums
    it doesn't hold a candle. Its Metallica mainstream hit which doesn't sound like thrash metal, more like grove metal
    (which made money at that time). Truly feels like a sellout album.`
    Highlight Tracks Include:

      Enter Sandman
      Sad But True
      The Unforgiven

    4.1 /10



    The Battle Of Los Angeles



    The Battle Of Los Angeles is Rage Against the Machine's 1999 album being more Hip Hop esque in comparison to the preceding albums.
    De La Rocha's vocals are astounding on this album, the yelling truly must take skill. This album is, what do you know, rageful.
    Overall enjoyable, not the best, but good.
    Highlight Tracks Include:

      Gurellia Radio
      Testify
      Calm Like A Bomb

    7.5 /10



    Heaven Or Las Vegas



    Heaven Or Las Vegas is the 1990 album released by the Cocteau Twins. The dream pop stars came out of the "Underground" with this album.
    This album is super accessible, has great production and sharp, yet powerful vocals. The album has a really
    great sound to it almost sounding like theres bells. The album isnt anything super special as of now and has some "shrieky" songs.
    Overall great, easy to listen too experimental album.
    Highlight Tracks Include:

      Iceblink Luck
      Cherry Colored Funk
      Heaven Or Las Vegas

    7.9/10



    IGOR



    Tyler, The Creator's 2019 Synth-Pop Neo-Soul album in my opinion is one of the greatest albums ever made.
    This album sounds like nothing I've ever heard or will hear again and its amazing. The production is nothing but
    astounding. The story of the album is emotional, raw and well versed through the lyrics. Overall an
    astounding album, in my opinion the best album of that decade.
    Highlight Tracks Include:

      WHATS GOOD
      IGOR'S THEME
      GONE GONE/THANK YOU

    10/10



    MM..FOOD

    OK Human

    River's vocals are on point and sound meloedic. This is one of
    the best Weezer albums as of late and its quite an enjoyable listen
    Highlight Tracks Include:

      Aloo Gobi
      Grapes Of Wrath
      Bird With A Broken Wing

    8.1/10
