What do you call cheese that's not yours?

Nach-yo Cheese!

You see, this is funny because of the play on words on 'Nacho Cheese':

([na·cho cheese] = The sauce that gives a plate of nachos so much appeal, nacho cheese is flavorful blend of melted cheese that is often combined with a few light spices and chopped or sliced chili peppers.

While it is a sauce, nacho cheese is considered cheese by some. In the joke, the cheese that's not yours is 'Nach-yo cheese' as it sounds like not yo cheese.



How do you make a tissue dance?

You put a little "Boogie" on it!

You see, this joke is funny because 'Boogie' had many different meanings

One of them is another meaning for 'boogers':

([boog·er] = a piece of dried nasal mucus.)

a boogie can also be used to describe 'dancing':

([dan·cing] = the activity of dancing for pleasure or in order to entertain others.)

the joke saying "You put a little Boogie on it" on how to make a tissue dance. Shows him saying both boogie in the dance way, and boogie in the booger way because you blow your nose with a tissue



What did the fish say when he swam into a wall

Dam!

You see, this joke is funny because of the response from the fish.

When the fish runs into the wall, he yells out "damn":

([damn] = expressing anger, surprise, or frustration.)

And though he uses it as a expression of anger from bumping into the wall. it's spelled out like "dam" Which is most likely the wall the fish bumped into.

([dam] = a barrier constructed to hold back water and raise its level, forming a reservoir used to generate electricity or as a water supply.)

What's red and bad for your teeth?

A brick!

You see, this joke is funny because this guy probably broke all his teeth trying to eat a 'brick':

([brick] = a small rectangular block typically made of fired or sun-dried clay, used in building.)

Did you hear about the italian chef who died?

He pasta-way!

You see, this joke is funny because of the way they phrase the chef dying. It's said that he 'passed away':

([passed away] = die)

But the way they spelled it 'pasta-way' is because of how italians chefs (and italians in general) are stereotyped to be the best cooks, especially in 'pasta' and 'pizza':

([piz·za] = a dish of Italian origin consisting of a flat, round base of dough baked with a topping of tomato sauce and cheese, typically with added meat or vegetables.)

So a snake walks into a bar, and the bartender says...

How did you do that!?!

You see, this joke is funny because the reptile we are talking about is a 'snake':

([snake] = a long limbless reptile which has no eyelids, a short tail, and jaws that are capable of considerable extension. Some snakes have a venomous bite.)

But becuase snakes have no legs, theres no way it could walk into the bar. The confused bartender then asks the snake "How did you do that!?!" as it should be impossible.
