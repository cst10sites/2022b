Soccer

What is it?

Soccer is arguably the most viewed and played sport on Earth. Soccer is a sport that consists on scoring goals by kicking a ball into the opposing team's net. Everytime a goal is scored your team recieves a point. The match will end after 90 minutes and the winner will be the team with the most goals.

Soccer was invented many centuries ago, but modern soccer originated in England in the 19th Century. After soccer became popular in London, the Football Association was founded. The FA decided on what the rules of the game should be e.g. The Goalkeeper is the only player that can use their hands. After 1850, is approximately when the sport became popular and many people started to play it. Soccer was very popular among industrial workers who would play soccer after their afternoon shifts. Some workers wouldn't even play, but just watch since it was very entertaining. The average number of spectators in FA matches was around 8,000, but the number grew explosively after the outbreak of World War I.

Originally, the FA didn't allow teams to pay their players, but teams started doing it because they wanted their line-ups to have better players. This partially led to teams charging money for admission to the games they played.The FA was later replaced by the Football League, which did allow their teams to pay their players and compete against eachother. After this, soccer became a profit-making business. The owners of the teams were usually business owners who saw their team as an opportunity to make money

The creation of the Football League led to more leagues being created around Europe like the Bundesliga in Germany. The creation of the FA and the Football League led to the expansion of soccert to the rest of the world, and today there are soccer leagues in every continent.
