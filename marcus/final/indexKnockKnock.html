Knock Knock

Tank

You're welcome

You see, this joke is funny because it's a play on words with 'Thank you':

([thank you] = a polite expression used when acknowledging a gift, service, or compliment, or accepting or refusing an offer.)

After you say "tank you" (Thank you), I am to respond "You're welcome" as the typical responce when someone thanks you. By the way

Knock Knock

Who's there?

A pileup!

A pileup who?

A pile of poo!

You see, this joke is funny because it's 'bathroom humour':

([bath·room hu·mor] = humor that is characterized by coarse references to bodily functions.)

A pileup sounds like "a pile of p" in which the who adds the two O's at the end (oo) to make A pile of poo!

Knock Knock

Who's there?

Cow says

Cow says who?

No, a cow says moo!

You see, this joke is funny because of its way talking about 'Cows':

([cow] = a fully grown female animal of a domesticated breed of ox, kept to produce milk or beef.)

While a cow says "moo" in real life, in the joke the person repeats "Cow says who?" to which they are corrected (No, a cow says moo!)

Knock Knock

Who's there?

Luke

Luke who?

Luke through the peep hole and find out!

You see, this joke is funny because of its play on words with 'look':

([look] = direct one's gaze toward someone or something or in a specified direction.)

While one would say "Look through", he said "Luke through", using the name he said earlier in the joke.
